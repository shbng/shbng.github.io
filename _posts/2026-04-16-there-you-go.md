8 minutes past twelve to*day*, I received an update from the mentorship team at SGB. Rejection :). The journey around this application was a growing experience! Not gonna hide, it's sad, and I have been spiraling down ever since. But let me try to pause that and note a few things I am proud of first :).

## Things that made me smile, things that pulled me tangentially

Firstly, I really leaned into the ideas around Science Gallery Bengaluru. I heard a lot of talks Dr. Jahnavi Phalkey and yeah, it was very connecting, coherent and revealing. I looked into conversation around <span class="courier">QUANTUM</span> at the SGB London, where the exhibit had been running over the course of last one year. For the first time, I took the opportunity to learn the narratives around art + science together. And learned that SGB India stands as a collective of not just those narratives but also beyond - recognising the siloes within which much of the academic education happens in India.

This was my entry point. In realising that there could be more human narratives around science. That, even if you had bitter acadmemic experiences/trajectory due to a mismatch of values, there's hope. So, SGB Bengaluru reignited that hope.

<span class="courier-code"><span>A cultural conversation around science...</span></span>

Ah, the beauty of these ideas.

I had been thinking about applying to this programme more than one month before the deadline. I had many doubts to proceed, but I had many encouraging reasons too. I had actually been trying to stitch my story, mostly via working on my site and blogs before applying. After doing that to some imperfect extent, I moved ahead after a fair share of doubts. Then I really started looking into SGB and the ideas around it as I said.

So, the next thing that makes me smile is my tiny progress in this *stitching*. This was a new space I was entering. A space for the liberal ideas. A space for serious and also joyfully curious*.

For me, it was about making my story more transparent to these lenses. I understood many of my values, struggles, and strengths through this lens. Then, I spent a lot of time thinking. Actually, it was in the times when I didn't actively spend time thinking I realised many links - they just kept coming. It all started to come together.

So yeah, I am proud of the fact that this journey lead me to figure a side of me, that I hadn't clearly before! It was quite an intellectualy journey. But interestingly, as I mention it often, it was also a critically creative experience too. More importantly, it was a creative process.

Zooming things out a little, I think this is a second milestone in my current break year. Second in the category of *survival & aligning with the society* aka career :p. The first one was the one month period around the AGQ-CDT application. There too I had to stitch a lot of academic strengths and values. There too I had quite an intellectual process in understanding applications and PhDs. There too I put my best foot forward.

So, the third thing to be proud of. So, it's now two times that I did it! I tried to put my best foot forward in the things I really believe in. I tried everything I can to stitch, talk, listen, express, doubt, apply, hope, and take a leap...

The clarity that came with these experiences is immense. I am indeed proud to figure a lot about these two sides of me. I just hope it all comes together sometime, that it rescues me someday.


There's something that came to me when I was aligning myself with SGB. 

<span class="courier">
I spent my last five years in a rather linear way, sticking to just math, physics - the efforts to studying these abstract topics and driving pleasure from them. I did nothing much apart from these, in a hollistic/liberal sense. This might be due to many reasons but the non-linearity of the subjects I was interested adds to this. The values I was stubborn about sticking to adds to this. My incompetency at many places adds to this. So, although I was very linear in the physical efforts, the ideas with which I was pursuing were very very non-linear. I was emphasizing on many human aspects. To be able to teach and communicate ideas were always the things I was aiming towards. It involved bringing in a certain artistic sense to science. But again, the efforts weere very linear. I had to spend a lot more time than necessary to meet those goals, not just because of "my" goals, but also, let's say 'because of the mismatch in the values of the system I was part of'. <br> But, apparently even those linear efforts didn't pay off immediately. The time, the efforts, the hard work... I then realised, <span class="yellow-highlight">a shorter path to academia involves a level of disconnect with many human values.</span> Atleast for me. And I didn't want to disconnect myself from those. I was not able to either. So, I decided to take a break, accepting a longer path towards academia by allowing myself to stay connected with some of the human things I wanted to bring to/get out of academia. That's where SGB also comes in as a space to nurture some of these liberal ideas, these human values, connecting rigorous siloed out academic narratives to more human endeavors...</span>
