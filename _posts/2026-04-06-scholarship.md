I might update my site regarding these. But I am going to be honest here. I will be quite liberal about it. Just because I have been part of a less liberal education and support, doesn't mean I shouldn't reach for the more culturally connected narratives/careers. It just requires some work and talking with the people who are exactly doing that. I know it's hard for others to believe in my process. But the moment I try to not make hard decisions, I fall back. I will have learnings from that yes, but I will be stuck in the middle forever. I have learned so many things in the past two years, and even from the current break. This break for me is honestly a period of isolation, to bring out what I have, after spending more than 5 years in a limited system. And as I said I am finding tangential hope and love that can pull me out of the isolation and give me a chance to show who I am. Until I find such a space which respects me for who I am, I am going to fight. May be it's hard to believe again when I say this, but I am also trying to keep a social minimum to survive. So I will keep trying to meet halfway with the society. But I can't escape thinking about social change either. I really really don't want to invest my energy into explaining myself to others or trying to do things that I don't fully believe in. Instead I am going to improve myself in the aspects where I know I can do better. I care about what you think, so I find myself investing this energy to explain it to you.


So, I just don't want you to feel that I am not interacting with anyone. I am in a period of isolation to hone my skills. But I am reading, and listening to so many people, talking about things. It's those people I want to interact with, and I will put myself out there to be able to do that.

Please excuse me for my insecurities regarding these. I have an ambition. But I think staying detached is the only way I can work on my career right now without limiting myself. 

That said, I am indeed talking to friends and in a capacity that I am always trying to increase! It always surprises me how good it feels to talk to people.
