## ACT - II - I did do a good job

Let me narrate my thesis direction here. The primary goal was eventually to tackle a reasonably novel theoretical computation using the tools of AdS/CFT correspondence. So my first semester on this project included learning these tools. I focused on geometry of the Anti-de Sitter space, and Conformal Field theory. During the second semester, I read about the correspondence and established key links between the two main objects.  It was an interestingly challenging exercise to navigate physics literature. The loose (pragmatic in their own ways) arguments and everything. I am proud of it acctually, to have navigated through that and settle on a good level of udnerstanding appreciating the nuacnes and getting ready for the next steps. So, it was time to tackle a computation. My advisor had an idea to compute. He sent me a paper that is directly relevant to the computation, wihcih provides the motivation to cahange a few things and extract some physics. It is during this point I finally come to peace with my inclination towards more mathematical research than research based on vague arguments. Of course the vaieness is relative. These are more vague to a mathematician, these are only highly pragmatic and rich for physicists.It's natural that I felt this way because all ym undergrad years, I had a inclination towards mathematical rigor and setup  over physcial language. 

Upon discussing these issues with my advisor, we thought it would be good to connect to the literature of mathematics in these areas. 


First was the compactness issues of Einstein manifolds related to the confromal boundary and AdS geometry. 

Second was about mathematical conformal field theory.

Both these works involved primarily a solid language of Riemannian geometry. So it was only natural for me to advacnce my differential geometry knowledge further and discuss AdS/CFT in that language. 


So I started doing that, I read Riemannian Geometry. I think it's a real deal. It took the rest of the semetster to do that. 

While I was learning Riemannian geometry, I also focused on how AdS/CFT interacts with mathematics. I learned about conformal geometry, conformal invariants, Fefferman Graham's formalism about construction of these invariants. So in this sense, I was heading towards presenting AdS/CFT as setup for computing conformal invariants. This was really exciting because, there was a global decomposition of conformal invariants predicted by physicists. Which was highly non-trivial mathematically and was only proved completely by a student of Feffarman in 2012. 

There is a lot of intersection between math and physics around manifold invariants. Gauge theory is a very solid example of this. So, my backgground here is bery strong in this sense. I am going to expand my thesis a little to include a potential calculation about these conformal invariants. Conformal anomalies are again at the root of this discussion. One can actually cpompute these anomalies using AdS/CFT. So can we do that nicely? Something I am going to do in discussion with my advisor soon.
