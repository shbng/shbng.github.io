I had my free Root Cause Analysis session today in the morning. A doctor from Granimals team joined online, asked about my history and location of the pain. Then she checked my movement patterns and pains.


Eliminated Meniscus related pains. Showed the heel loaded knee extension + bend pain.

Then she checked my form during 90 degree squats + deep squats, forwards and backward lunges, and bulgarian split squats. And there we go, all my workouts have been going quad heavy! More broadly, anterior chain heavy. So she explained me that my posterior chain is very weak and I tried to load mostly anteriorly. This causes the quads and the tendon below patella to get strained. A case of patellar tendonitis, she explained. I corrected and tried to repeat the squats, but I could see I had trouble! I was weak on the posterior side.

Also, during Bulgarian Split squats, you load 90-10 (front-back legs) only during quad focused variation! I had my upper body and form cues lean towards glute focused variation, but the leg-load I aimed at was 90%-10%. So, I didn't feel a thing in my glutes, or hamstrings. Instead my quads were getting overburdened, and can even feel some strain lately on my right outer quad (Vastus lateralis).


First goal is to isolate the posterior chain and work exclusively on that for some time, so that I learn to activate them without a chance to rely on quads.

PS: **A word on why strength training matters**


### Level 1:

Poor posture effects us everyday. We tend to get back pains easily if we sit for long and work. We try maintaining a good posture but ah, it feels so artificial! It feel so *draining*. That's where strength training kicks in. We strengthen the different muscles in our body to support this process. We don't have to try to maintain that posture always, the body just loads the weight onto the stronger muscles and it feels natural.  

That was more about upper body. But there are things happening in the lower body too. For example, take hips. Sitting cause tight and weak hip flexor muscles. And then, when we start walking or running or trekking or any physical activity for that matter, it's very easy for those muscles to be strained, tight and achey.

And then comes the quad and calf muscles. If they are weak, our knees are naturally more burdened, even without a lot of physical activity. Osteo-arthritis (knee joint degeneration due to loss of cartilage) is very common in India. It's due to both, low protein consumption and no resistance training to strengthen the muscles actively. Quads and calves (along with hips and glutes) take away lot of that load off the knees! It's beautiful how our body has these collective workers. And we have that opportunity to make use of them! So yeah, over the past couple months, watching my parents struggle with osteo-arthritis and weak leg muscles, pushes me even further to make sure I strengthen. It also makes sense why Indian population in their old age are weaker in mobility. Huge muscle loss and no resistance training, yep.


### Level 2:

Any physical activity requires a free range of motion at various links of our body. It becomes essential to strengthen every link!! 

Ah, I never thought about hip flexors, hid abductors, and hip adductors at all. Not Hamstrings either honestly.

But now I know. To maintain a good form, to be able to do the activity we want to do for longer and better, we must train.

And ah, it feels so good to strengthen these weak links. To know that, in long-term this matters and put me in good shape. To travel and move even with age.


But we can never expect an injury sometimes. We can never prepare for everything. We just hope our best :). Give ourselves a better chance. Sometimes the only way we learn is by falling :).
