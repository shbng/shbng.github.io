> Field Farm: A lifelong companion or <em>a human touch</em> to the depths of mathematics and physics. A companion which recalls and reconnects intuition across the topics in mathematics and physics. Simply put, it tells the story of what's happening -- talks through the theory :). Mostly a personal effort to consolidate things. The emphasis is again on the <em>story</em>, not proofs of all the facts! Every communicator has their own voice - in aspects of rigor, human touch, art, creativity and what not. Through this I am finding (farming) mine :).  

Throughout the five years of my undergraduation, I've had many scholarly temptations to exposite many beautiful tiny concepts that came across. But there was a lack of time, for even exploring and understanding the syllabus was quite a challenge, for reasons related to curriculum, teaching and personal interests. 

Now, during this break year, I want to write articles, and even expositories on different concepts in math and physics. Right now I am in a Core Math reading phase until December. And as usual, many tiny topics come up and capturing them with words feels just nice. It's mostly a personal effort to recall, remember and communicate the intuition and abstract beauty. I belive it will help me in future too when I perhaps land a TAship or even a research position! 

After december Physics Core reading will start, and so will the attempts to consoliate physics with mathematics. 

Articles being released right now are very elementary, but neverthless give a strong introduction to the funamental ideas in mathematics. So I am sharpening my curisoity and writing skills, mostly experimenting and keeping it raw and *putting in more reps*. Hopefully when the time comes to the more advanced topics in math and physics, I get better at it :).


There are many, many topics that I have in mind to write about. And within an year these should roll out one by one. I won't list the topic names here though. I will wait for the time to come. For now, basic analysis and algebra articles should keep coming weekly.
