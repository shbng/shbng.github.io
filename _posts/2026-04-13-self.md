I am currently reading (and/or listening to) *Walden*, listening to A*natomy for Runners*, and reading *Fixing Science in India*. I often feel lonely when I read. As if I am missing out on the world while I spend time reading.

The last time I read regularly was around December 2024 which continued till early 2025 as well. I had the same feelings back then and I noticed how I felt a disconnection from the world because I was reading so much and alone. I found audiobooks comforting because, I can hear a human voice. It's less lonely. But that felt eerie too sometimes. I was also not using social media back then.

So, may be I can try a couple of things here. What if,

$\diamond$ I continue to stay in touch with the media and society through Instagram - so that I don't get disconnected.


$\diamond$ I express bite sized things I liked in the books and put it out somewhere. May be on instagram, or right here - so that I don't feel lonely.

$\diamond$ I disconnect from *myself* a little - so that I become more in touch with the society, and the world!

The third one struck me when I was reading *Fixing Science in India* few minutes back, and there were thoughts racing...tempting me to do something different than this, something more directed towards my *goals*. Something *'product'ive*. Ah, yep. It's so tempting to produce something, be it work, or another hobby, that can be *displayed*. Always to produce. Producing has become a way to feel good. And there's always consumption. I do note that I have also gotten more closer towards passive/short form consumption. But there is definitely that active and slow part to it in many ways. But reading a book that's not math, that slow burn, has been a little challenging these days. 


Recently, I did read *Quiet*, quite a bit :). But eventually I caught up in my fast pace. I do feel that, to go slow demands many pieces to come together. I mean, with my context of *a pathless path* and the uncertainty it carries, it's hard to go slow. Especially considering I didn't develop a habit of reading a lot in childhood. And during the difficult times, the other form of consumtpion give that solace, easy distractions from the very deep doubts and works. Escaping that intellectual space, so that reading the book, slowly, doesn't feel familiar to work.

But I really like the idea behind the book, *Fixing Science in India: A Socioeconomic Perspective*. I heard about this book the other week and it felt so relevant. I have been trying to increase my political grasp and everything, and with SGB, and my own crafting of personal path around academia, learning a lot about academia, sciences and everything, so this was just great news...

I only read the preface so far, and listened to some parts of the conversation. Let's see how it goes. 

Regarding *Walden*. I bought this book a couple of years ago. I am finding it hard to read most of the time, unless I have a lot of energy... But I found the audiobook on spotify to be decent. It's a good listen, especially since the book is a lot of retelling about HDT's stay near Walden. I will try to listen to it more.

I didn't want to buy *Anatomy for Runners*. So I am sticking to the audiobook I got for free on audible.


And back to the third point about disconnecting from *self* a little. I tend to get very self-obsessed and I want to see what happens if I let go of some *obsession to constantly work on my self* or *prove myself* and stay in touch with the other callings, like simply reading books.
